Bio

Starting my career as a graphic and type designer, I co-founded a design studio called Grotesk.cc together with Reto Moser, ran a nomadic art Gallery/Off-Space called Raum NoGrotesk.cc, and worked for the internal communications department of the university of the arts in Bern, Switzerland.

In 2012 I joined the branding agency Eclat in Zürich where I worked mostly on branding and financial communications projects for various Swiss companies. In my role, I was responsible for developing creative concepts, executing the design, and advising and consulting the clients.

Three years later, in 2015, I moved to Berlin, Germany to pursue a Master of Business Administration focused on Innovation and Change Management. The topics covered during my studies range from Accounting, Business Strategy and Innovation, Leadership, Strategic Marketing to Crosscultural Management. I finished my studies with a Masters thesis about Change Management in remote SMEs.

In 2016 I went on to establish prolog.work a studio for design and technology together with Daniel Eytan Schneider. We worked for a wide range of private companies and public institutions. Our capabilities range across exhibition design, website development, branding, and communication and business strategy. Prolog was relaunched in 2020 with two additional partners, Tom Walsh and Can Kaya, and I remain a member of the board of directors and freelance strategist.

Besides my work I regularly teach typography at the School of Design in Bern and Bienne, Switzerland.

Since 2018 I work as an Account and Managing Director at the Swiss type foundry Grilli Type. Besides offering retail typefaces, we’re also a service provider. We create completely custom typeface systems for companies like for example, Pinterest, WeTransfer, and Marshalls.

I live in Berlin, Germany with my family.
